Whale sightings and chlorophyll a concentrations by month in the Santa Barbara Channel in 2016






This map visually demonstrates how whale sightings relate to the concentration of chlorophyll a in the Santa Barbara Channel. 2016 monthly humpback whale sighting data points are derived from a larger whale sighting dataset spanning 20 years. The light green shading indicates low concentrations of chlorophyll a and dark green shading indicates high concentrations of chlorophyll a. There is a general lack of oceanagraphic data and minimal available data on whale populations' distributions. Using whale sighting data collected by a highly-trained group of citizen scientists, we see an interesting relationship between chlorophyll a concentrations and monthly whale sightings, shown for 2016. Further understanding of this relationship could better predict where to find humpbacks in the Santa Barbara Channel of California as well as elsewhere around the world. Using a habitat suitability model for endangered whales species in the Channel, Scales et al.(2017) found whales preferred intermediate chlorophyll a concentrations, corroborating our results.


Metadata:
Humpback Whale Sightings Source: Channel Islands National Marine Sanctuaries Naturalist Corps (vector points)
Chlorophyll Concentration Source: NASA GSFC OceanColor (raster data)
Spatial resolution = 4 km2 (averaged annually)
Temporal resolution = 2007-2016 (averaged annually)
